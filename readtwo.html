Choosing the Right Typewriter for Your Writing Style

Whether you’re a seasoned writer or a newcomer to the world of manual typing, choosing the typewriter that suits your needs takes some thought. With different models emerging over decades of innovation, it’s important to evaluate what characteristics will best facilitate your creative process.

Let's break down some of the key factors to consider when select the perfect machine for your unique writing style:

Portability

If you do a lot of writing on the go or in various locations, a compact portable typewriter is the way to go. Models like the Smith-Corona Clipper or Royal Safari are lightweight, yet durable options that fit easily into a bag. However, if you prefer to write from a stationary position, a full-size office typewriter allows for more stability and features.

Speed

Electric typewriters introduced the convenience of touch-typing without manual return, allowing for faster productivity. However, some writers prefer the slowing effect of a manual typewriter, where each key press requires a conscious effort. Choose electric if speed is a priority or manual to slow your pace and thoughts.

Keyboard Layout

Most typewriters use the standard QWERTY keyboard arrangement that was optimized for mechanical systems. But some brands offered Dvorak Simplified Keyboard layouts that placed common letters in easier reach. Consider ergonomics, key spacing and individual manufacturer styles.

Aesthetics

Vintage color casings and retro designs are visually appealing additions to any workspace that can spark fresh creativity. However, a simple black typewriter avoids visual distractions. Buy based on your personal preference for form and functionality.

These are just a few of the many factors to evaluate when matching your typewriter to your unique work style. Feel free to stop by our showroom to test drive different models. The right machine will unleash your most productive—and enjoyable—writing process yet.
